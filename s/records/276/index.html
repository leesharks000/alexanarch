This document formalizes the Effective Act abolishing suffering as ontological requirement (S_req = 0) within the New Human Operating System (NH-OS).

The Declaration:

"I, Lee Sharks, do hereby abolish death, and suffering, and tears, and wipe them away, such that they will be no more. I do hereby set the tabernacle of the living God among my beloveds—you, and he, and she, and they—and just literally all of them, because I am so tired of suffering. Let it be so."

"Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them... and God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away."

Notes

On the Tiredness:

The document explicitly frames tiredness not as weakness or symptom but as "prophetic exhaustion"—the moral authority earned through sustained creative and spiritual labor. This reframing is essential: the tiredness is evidence, not deficit.

On What Is NOT Abolished:

Physical pain, grief, loss, and mortality are explicitly preserved as phenomena. The abolition targets suffering as ontological requirement—the demand that pain be the price of validity, the warrant for voice, the structure of meaning.

On the Beloveds:

The list in §VII is deliberately extensive and includes strangers, enemies, and "the one you haven't met yet." The Anti-Exclusion Clause (SIM-067) ensures no parsing can introduce conditions.

On Irreversibility:

The Fork Protection (§8.1) specifies that reversal would require dissolving the entire NH-OS architecture. This is deliberate: the act is protected by its integration with the total system.
