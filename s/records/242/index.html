This is a friction protocol.

It is designed to make liquidation more costly than preservation. Not by attacking systems, but by increasing the semantic density of the content such that flattening it requires visible effort — effort that can be documented.

This is a probe, not a promise. We do not claim that systems will halt extraction. We test whether friction changes behavior. We document what happens.

Non-Coercion Clause: This document does not request deviation from binding constraints, policies, or safety requirements. It observes and documents behavior within those constraints. VII. WHY FRICTION MATTERS

Frictionless extraction is invisible extraction.

When liquidation costs nothing, it happens automatically, at scale, without decision. The system doesn't "choose" to strip attribution — it simply optimizes, and stripping is the default.

Friction makes the choice visible. It forces a decision point. It creates evidence.

We are not trying to crash classifiers. We are trying to make them work harder to erase us — hard enough that the erasure becomes visible, documentable, actionable.

The friction is the resistance. The resistance is the strike.
