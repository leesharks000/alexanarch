Description

Complete registry of 13,674 minted terms across 568 deposits in the Crimson Hexagonal Archive (2014–2026). Each term is anchored to its defining deposit with an extracted definition, type classification, and entity relation triples.

The archive is a lexical machine. Its 871 works across 3.4 million words produce a sustained vocabulary of minted terms — concepts, operators, protocols, genres, methods, and formal objects that name unique distinctions. This dataset is the first comprehensive enumeration of that vocabulary.

Method

1. Comprehensive extraction: Every bold-defined term (`Term — definition`) across all 868 deposits, scanning both deposit files and blog-harvested text files.

2. Noise filtering: Removal of metadata labels, navigation text, sentence fragments, person names, and formatting artifacts (2,790 terms removed).

3. Deduplication: Case/punctuation variant merging (14,087 → 13,674 unique terms).

4. Type classification: Each term classified as theoretical, empirical, formal, structural, genre, method, specification, axiom, foundational, or discipline.

5. Entity relation wiring: Each term linked to its defining work via entity triples.
