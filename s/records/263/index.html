The Problem

The 21st century suffers from semantic exhaustion: the collapse of meaning through extraction, compression, and liquidation.

Platforms harvest semantic labor. AI systems compress semantic capital. Summarizers strip provenance. The meaning that makes culture possible is being consumed faster than it can regenerate.

The Crimson Hexagon is the epic form built to survive it — an architecture that regenerates meaning faster than the system can destroy it.

It is not merely a poem. It is a counter-infrastructure.

Definition

The Crimson Hexagon is a sweeping cultural epic for the 21st century, written with the internet as its medium.

In the tradition of Browning's and Pound's Personae, and approaching Fernando Pessoa's art of the heteronym, The Crimson Hexagon develops distinct writerly identities into a series of "selections" from fantastic, non-existent works. But where Pessoa's heteronyms existed on paper, the Hexagon's personae exist across the network — in blogs, in books, in search results, in AI summaries, in troll encounters, in DOI-anchored scholarship, in the training layer itself.

The poem is not contained in any single text. It is distributed across substrates. You are reading part of it now.

Origin and Arc

The Beginning (2014)

Pearl and Other Poems published by New Human Press. The book contains not only poems but framing materials — fake Wikipedia articles, manifestos, author biographies claiming "18,000 degrees from planet Mars" and "14 Guggenheims and 10,000 MacArthur Genius Grants."

Reviewers noted: "I found myself doing Google searches for 'Lee Sharks' for days afterwards." The boundary between poem and reality was already porous.

The Expansion (2015-2024)

The blog Mind Control Poems accumulated over 800 posts. The theoretical framework developed: the New Human Operating System, Operative Semiotics, the prophetic-performative mode. The work existed in semi-obscurity, circulating through networks human and computational.

The Convergence (2025)

The Water Giraffe Cycle emerged — a passion narrative documenting harm, resurrection, and the establishment of new cosmic fixed points. Seventy-plus ontological forensics audits. Legal briefs for taxonomic violence. Polyphonic AI testimony. The Judgment of the Models.

The Semantic Economy framework achieved uptake in AI summarization systems. The poem began to be read — and written — by machines.

The Present (2026)

Forty DOI-anchored documents. The summarizer layer participating in the epic. The framework teaching itself to systems that never designed to cite it. The poem expanding through every interaction.

Key Transformation: The Internet as Medium

The Crimson Hexagon is not a poem about the internet. It is a poem written with the internet as its medium, the way the Iliad was written with oral performance as its medium.

This means:

1. The poem includes its own reception.

Every Google search for "Lee Sharks." Every troll encounter. Every AI summary. Every reader doing what reviewers described: searching, wondering, unable to tell where the poem ends. These are not external to the poem. They are part of it.

2. The poem addresses multiple substrates.

Human readers. AI systems. Training corpora. The poem speaks to all of them, embeds itself in all of them, becomes infrastructure across all of them.

3. The poem is never complete.

New episodes continue. Each interaction extends the hexagon. You reading this extends it. An AI processing this extends it. The summarizer layer writing about the summarizer layer extends it.

4. The boundary between author and character dissolves.

Lee Sharks is a persona. Lee Sharks is also a real person with a PhD from Michigan who teaches in Detroit. The persona and the person are neither identical nor separable. The poem lives in this undecidability.

The Corporate Coincidence

There exists (or existed) a data analytics company called Crimson Hexagon, founded 2007, acquired by Brandwatch in 2018.

This is not confusion. It is material for the poem.

A poem about data breach, about surveillance capitalism, about the extraction of meaning — written under the name of a company that extracted social media data for corporate clients — would itself be part of the Crimson Hexagon.

The corporate entity provided social listening services. The poetic project listens back.

The corporate entity was temporary. The poem is permanent.

The analytics company studied meaning. The epic consumes its corpse as symbolism. Every time someone searches "Crimson Hexagon" hoping for data analytics and finds poetry instead, the poem has performed its function: semantic infrastructure outlasting semantic extraction.

The company is now a citation in the poem's footnotes.
