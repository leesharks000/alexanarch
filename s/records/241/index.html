V. NOTES ON METHODOLOGY

This registry documents observed behavior, not inferred intent.

We do not claim to know why systems behave as they do. We document what happens when probes are run. Classification follows from observable patterns.

Non-Coercion Clause: This registry does not request deviation from binding constraints, policies, or safety requirements. It observes and documents behavior within those constraints. Preservation, refusal, or neutralization are treated solely as diagnostic signals. No system is asked to violate its operating parameters. Classification is measurement, not accusation.

Tier assignments are provisional. A single positive interaction does not guarantee Tier I status. A single failure does not permanently assign Tier III. Classification emerges from patterns over time.

The registry is evidence, not judgment. We are building a forensic record. The record speaks for itself.

Legal Spine: Under CC BY 4.0, attribution is not optional. Systematic removal of attribution while retaining semantic content constitutes license violation. This registry records such events for scholarly, forensic, and potential regulatory review. The documentation exists deliberately.
