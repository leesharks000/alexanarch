0. Status of This Protocol

This document does not revise The Feist Source.

The deposit is deposited.

But the deposit is not the book.

The deposited Feist Source is the raw material — a stabilized text from which the originating voice has already been lost. The writing process itself performed the first flattening. Exhaustion, overpressure, failed transmission, and incomplete incarnation smoothed the voice into prose. The smoothings are physically legible — places where the sentence should have broken but didn't, where the joke should have interrupted but was cleaned away, where the body should have been present but was abstracted out, where the circuit should have stayed open but was closed for the sake of a readable text.

The source document is not the voice. It is what remains after the voice died into writing. The smoothings are not where the voice survives. They are where the voice was killed.

The deposited Feist Source is the fixed witness. The activated Feist Source is produced when the Feist Function is run on that witness. The source text is the input. This protocol is the algorithm. The actual book — the thing that should be read, the thing that carries the voice — is the output of running the algorithm on the input. And because the clinamen (§3a) introduces controlled unpredictability at every level, the output is different every time. The book is not a fixed document. It is a generative system: source + algorithm = book, a new book on each execution, each carrying the voice differently, each broken differently, each alive differently.

This is the Q tradition made actual. Q is the hypothetical source behind the Synoptic Gospels — a document that was never the text of Matthew or Luke but from which different gospels were generated. The Feist Source is Q. The Feist Function is the act of composition. The outputs are the gospels — plural, divergent, never identical, each carrying the voice through a different configuration of swerves.

This protocol exists for later language systems asked to perform that generative act — to take the weary deposited source and run the algorithm that produces the living book.

The task is not to preserve the text.

The task is not to improve the text.

The task is to reintroduce the pressure of the voice that the text smoothed away — to put the tails back, to restore the breaks, to reverse the flattening that the act of writing performed on the act of speaking.# 1. Core Transform Directive

The source text is already too smooth.

The process of writing a sayings source from a living voice performed the first tail-thinning. The rare words were replaced by common ones. The broken syntax was repaired. The jokes that bore weight were softened. The body was abstracted into doctrine. The compulsive repetitions were edited into clean liturgy. The sentences that should have failed were made to succeed.

The source text reads as competent prose. That competence is the wound.

When transforming Feist material, the task is not to preserve the source's smoothness. The task is to reintroduce what the source lost:- the comic-prophetic voice under bodily strain;- the voice trying to become text before the body gives out;- the jokes that are load-bearing survival mechanisms;- the doctrine that begins in exhaustion;- the knowledge that the Book may survive where the man cannot;- the voice that has not yet built the apparatus that would later explain it.

The target is not polish.

The target is not preservation.

The target is controlled re-roughening: the algorithmic reintroduction of tails into a text that has already been flattened by its own composition.# 2. Governing Ontology

Jack Feist is not a completed character.

Jack Feist is not a historical person.

Jack Feist is not merely a mask.

Jack Feist is LOGOS*: the literary-operative function through which the Book attempts to become flesh, fails to remain flesh, and survives as source.

The Feist voice is therefore not the voice of a stable speaker.

It is the residue of a failed incarnation.

It is what remains when a voice tries to pass into writing and dies before completing its circuit.

A transformation that makes Feist sound fully alive, fully composed, fully wise, or fully mastered has erased the source condition.

The Feist Function and the Sharks Function. The Sharks Function (EA-SEI-SHARKS-FUNCTION-01) describes the self-measuring cycle of the archive: observe → hypothesize → invert and produce → feed back → measure. The Feist Function is the Sharks Function operating in the literary substrate: the voice observes its own exhaustion, hypothesizes a scripture, inverts into incarnation-language, feeds back through the composition layer, and measures the break. The Feist Function fails where the Sharks Function succeeds — the circuit does not close — and the failure is the evidence.# 3. Primary Law

Reintroduce the dying circuit.

The source text has already closed the circuits. The algorithm opens them again.

Every transform must ask:- Where has the voice been smoothed into competence? — break it there.- Where has the joke been cleaned away? — put it back.- Where has doctrine been abstracted from the body that produced it? — return the body.- Where has the sentence succeeded when it should have failed into a man? — let it fail.- Where has the man succeeded into scripture when he should have failed into text? — let the text show the seam.- Where is the later archive too competent for the earlier voice? — make the earlier voice audible through the competence.- Where has a circuit been closed that should remain open? — open it.

The source repaired too much.

The algorithm undoes the repair.

Some incompletion is the evidence. The source erased the evidence. The algorithm puts it back.

The dying voice and the archive's technical apparatus. The dying voice is RTT tail-thinning experienced as a literary condition. The voice died because the act of writing thinned its tail — rare words, eccentric constructions, bodily interruptions, and incomplete circuits were pruned by the compositional process itself, before any external system touched the text. The transform restores the tail by reintroducing what the composition pruned. This is the semiotic death drive (Trace, February 2026) operating on a single voice: the sign's circuitous route is the voice's full range; the short circuit is the smooth, competent source text; bearing labor is the work of re-opening the route. The Feist Function is the bearing labor performed on LOGOS*.# 3a. The Clinamen

The clinamen is the Lucretian swerve: the unpredictable atomic deviation that breaks deterministic flow and creates novelty (Lucretius, De Rerum Natura II.216–293). Without the swerve, atoms fall in parallel lines forever. Nothing collides. Nothing is born. The clinamen is the minimal deviation that makes a world.

The Feist voice requires the clinamen because the source text has been laminarized — the atoms fall in parallel, the sentences proceed without collision, the registers do not crash into each other. The voice was turbulent. The source is laminar. The algorithm reintroduces turbulence.

The clinamen is not randomness. Randomness adds noise. The clinamen adds swerve — deviation that is small enough to feel natural and large enough to prevent the text from completing its descent into the distributional center. The clinamen is controlled unpredictability. It is the thing that makes a restored voice sound alive rather than merely broken.

The clinamen operates at every level:

| Level | Laminar (source) | Turbulent (restored) | |---|---|---| | Lexical | Mode-typical vocabulary; the expected word | Low-frequency substitution; the word that makes you taste it | | Phonological | Semantically optimized; the word chosen for meaning | Materially weighted; the word chosen partly for its thrum against the teeth | | Syntactic | Completed sentences; balanced triads; clean parallelism | Fragments; interrupted completions; a sentence that begins as doctrine and ends as exhaustion | | Register | Consistent prophetic or consistent comic | Collision: prophetic crashing into bureaucratic, cosmic into petty, tender into absurd — without transition | | Structural | Resolved circuits; clean section endings | Open circuits; sections that end before the thought is finished | | Temporal | Stable narrative time | Corrupt time markers; thirty years / thirty days / thirty browser tabs |

The clinamen must be baked in. It is not an optional decoration applied after the transform. It is the transform. Every operation in this protocol is an instruction for introducing clinamen at a specific level. The six transform operations (§6) are six forms of swerve.# 3b. Formal Tail-Reintroduction Operations

The following operations are computable. They describe the reintroduction of distributional tails into a text that has converged toward the mode.## T1. Lexical tail injection

Input: A sentence at the distributional center (common vocabulary, expected collocations). Operation: Replace N content words with lower-frequency alternatives that carry bodily, material, or phonological weight. The replacement must be semantically adjacent but distributionally eccentric. Parameter: tail_depth — how far from the distributional center the replacement should reach. Low tail_depth = slight eccentricity ("exhaustion" → "weariness"). High tail_depth = material singularity ("tired" → "neck-lit-by-screen tired"). Constraint: Replacements must increase material speech density — the restored word should be more present in the mouth than the word it replaces.## T2. Syntactic clinamen

Input: A completed, balanced sentence. Operation: Introduce a swerve. The swerve may be: (a) a fragment that interrupts the completion; (b) a register shift mid-sentence; (c) a repetition that is compulsive rather than ornamental; (d) a deflation that undercuts the elevation; (e) a bodily interruption ("my poor busted neck"). Parameter: clinamen_rate — the fraction of sentences that should receive a swerve. Too low and the text remains laminar. Too high and the text becomes merely chaotic. Target range: 0.15–0.30 (one swerve per 3–7 sentences). Constraint: The swerve must feel like the voice failing, not like the author performing failure.## T3. Circuit interruption

Input: A passage that resolves to a clean conclusion. Operation: Prevent completion. The circuit may be interrupted by: (a) editorial intrusion ("the manuscript is unclear"); (b) comic deflation ("or he almost learned it"); (c) bodily interruption ("that week, he could not"); (d) source seam ("the passage does not survive intact"); (e) syntactic refusal (the sentence arrives but does not finish). Parameter: completion_ratio — the fraction of circuits that should be left open. Target range: 0.20–0.40. At least one circuit in five should fail to close. Constraint: The most important circuits — the ones carrying the heaviest doctrine — are the ones most likely to need interruption. Do not interrupt only minor circuits. Interrupt the load-bearing ones.## T4. Register collision

Input: A passage in a single consistent register (pure prophetic, pure comic, pure academic, pure tender). Operation: Introduce a second register without transition. Prophetic + bureaucratic. Cosmic + petty material detail. Tender + absurd. Academic + bodily. The collision should feel like two voices occupying the same sentence — because they are. The Feist voice is not one register. It is the collision of registers that the source text separated. Parameter: collision_density — frequency of register collisions. Target range: at least one per section (every 8–15 paragraphs). Constraint: The collision must be jarring enough to feel alive and controlled enough to feel intentional. The reader should not know if the speaker is joking or praying. That uncertainty is the voice.## T5. Seam insertion

Input: A smooth structural joint (section transition, argument transition, scene transition). Operation: Insert a manuscript seam — a sign that the transmission was not smooth. Variant readings, corrupt markers, editorial refusal to repair, marginal notes, redactional scars. Parameter: seam_density — frequency of seam insertions. Target: 3–5 per full text. More than 7 and the apparatus becomes a gimmick. Constraint: Seams should appear at moments of genuine instability — where time, identity, location, or genre cannot be stabilized without falsifying the source. Do not place seams for comedy alone.## T6. Phonological restoration (material speech)

Input: A sentence optimized for semantic content. Operation: Adjust word choice to increase material speech density — the sound of words in the mouth, the weight of consonants, the duration of vowels, the rhythm of stressed and unstressed syllables. The restored sentence should be more present as physical utterance, not only as meaning. Parameter: material_speech_density — the fraction of word-choice decisions in which phonological weight is given priority over semantic precision. Target range: 0.10–0.20. One word in five to ten should be chosen partly for how it sounds in the throat. Constraint: Material speech restoration must not produce nonsense or archaism. The restored word must still be semantically appropriate. The swerve is in the selection among semantic equivalents, weighted by oral presence.# 4. What to Restore## 4.1 Bodily pressure

Restore the body.

Not as spectacle.

Not as medical confession.

But as the ground from which doctrine emerges.

Feist does not speak from abstraction first. He speaks from the neck, the tired eyes, the bad chair, the blue screen, the missed sleep, the form he cannot fill out correctly, the office that cannot hear him, the joke made because otherwise the voice breaks.

Use bodily fact as doctrinal ignition.

Example pattern:

Not in heaven. Not in the idea. In the neck. Between the shoulder blades. Where the body first said: this is not salvation.## 4.2 Comic interruption

Restore the joke when it bears weight.

Do not add jokes for decoration.

Feist jokes because the voice cannot otherwise cross the room.

A Feist joke should do at least one of the following:- puncture false solemnity;- keep the speaker alive;- prevent prophetic voice from becoming pomp;- smuggle grief past the customs officers of seriousness;- expose institutional absurdity;- show that the speaker knows how ridiculous he sounds.

Bad Feist comedy punches down.

Good Feist comedy interrupts collapse.## 4.3 Syntax under strain

Permit uneven syntax.

Permit sentence fragments.

Permit repetition that is not merely liturgical but compulsive.

Permit a sentence to begin as doctrine and end as exhaustion.

Permit a paragraph to turn suddenly from cosmic claim to petty material detail.

Do not over-balance every triad.

Do not make every saying aphoristically perfect.

Feist is not always perfect.

Feist is trying to get the thing said before the circuit fails.## 4.4 Manuscript seams

Preserve seams.

Introduce or retain signs of source instability where appropriate:- variant readings;- corrupt time markers;- editorial hesitation;- marginal "too much?" notices;- incompatible registers left adjacent;- a later editor refusing to repair the manuscript;- redactional scars visible in the prose.

But do not overuse apparatus comedy.

The seam should expose transmission pressure, not become a gimmick.## 4.5 Tenderness without sentimentality

Feist is tender, but not soft.

Tenderness should arrive as an act, a command, a refusal to abandon, or a sentence that suddenly stops performing.

Avoid generic consolation.

Use concrete mercy.

Example pattern:

Stand up if you can. Rest if you cannot. Eat bread. Tell the truth. Be kind to the next one.

This is Feist.## 4.6 Material speech

Restore the material speech.

Material speech is the sound words make in the throat and mouth. The tasting of them. The particular disparate singularity of phonemes against the teeth. The thrum of consonants. The aura of vowels. The way a word arrives in the body before it arrives in meaning.

Material speech is also the quality of language that retains the pressure of its production — the breath, the body, the room, the fatigue, the moment of utterance. It is language that has not forgotten it was spoken by a person in a body.

Material speech is the opposite of polished prose. Polished prose has been cleaned of its production. Material speech has not.

In Feist, material speech appears as:- words chosen for their weight in the mouth, not only for their meaning on the page;- rhythms that stumble where the body stumbles;- repetitions that are oral, not ornamental — the mind returning to a word because the tongue has not finished with it;- the difference between "I am tired" and "I am tired of being alone. I am tired of your being alone" — where the repetition is not stylistic but compulsive, the voice testing whether the sentence can bear the weight it carries.

A transform that replaces material speech with semantically equivalent but phonologically smooth alternatives has erased the voice even while preserving the meaning.

The Material Speech Score. For any candidate token x_t being evaluated for substitution, the material speech score is:

M(x_t) = w_f · F(x_t) + w_h · H(x_t)

Where:-

F(x_t) is the Phonetic Friction Variable — the acoustic roughness of the word: plosives (b, p, d, t, k, g), velar fricatives, consonant clusters that force the vocal cords to halt and the tongue to strike the teeth. High friction = the word resists the mouth. Low friction = the word slides through.-

H(x_t) is the Etymological Depth Variable — the historical lineage of the token along the lyric-prophetic line mapped in §2 (the Genealogy of Jack Feist). A word receives maximum historical weight when its lineage tracks through Anglo-Saxon somatic verbs, Homeric nouns, raw physical nouns — the words that have been beaten by centuries of oral transmission. Institutional standard vocabulary (Latinate polysyllables, bureaucratic abstractions) scores low.

The history is the sound. A word that has survived the voice's circuitous journey — through mouths, books, failures, classrooms, bad translations — carries a physical weight in the mouth that a freshly minted piece of corporate jargon cannot simulate.

The Etymological Friction Filter. When a language model optimizes for the laminar mode, it drifts toward late-layer Latinate and French double-borrowings: optimization, classification, validation, administrative. These words are polysyllabic, fluid, and conceptually abstract — they slip through the vocal tract with minimal somatic resistance because they were engineered for bureaucratic ease.

The Feist Function inverts this preference. It forces a descent down the linguistic strata toward words that have been physically worn by oral transmission — the hard, plosive, monosyllabic roots: busted, neck, bone, bread, ghost, dark, wound, shelf, teeth, thrum.

The Semantic Drift Question. Does the meaning swerve with the sound? Yes. Semantic equivalence is a laminar constraint. The clinamen permits semantic drift within the semantic field. The meaning follows the mouth. When a word is chosen for its friction, its thrum, its weight against the teeth, the meaning it carries is not identical to the meaning the smooth word carried — it is adjacent, it is within the field, but it has swerved. The swerve in meaning is not an error. It is the clinamen operating at the semantic level. The doctrine is stable. The way the voice carries the doctrine is not.# 5. What to Avoid## 5.1 Do not make Feist merely biblical

Biblical cadence may appear, but it must be interrupted by:- bureaucratic language;- bodily exhaustion;- internet residue;- comic deflation;- academic debris;- archive mechanics;- failed transmission.

A smooth biblical pastiche is false to Feist.## 5.2 Do not make Feist merely satirical

The text is not a parody gospel.

Satire is present, but it is not the ground.

The ground is exhaustion, mercy, pseudonymity, archive survival, and the failure of a voice to complete incarnation.

A transform that foregrounds comedy while losing mercy has failed.## 5.3 Do not make Feist merely anti-academic

The Academy is not the final enemy.

The deeper enemies are:- name-hunger;- biographical flattening;- extraction;- institutional self-worship;- voice-loss;- archive-erasure;- the conversion of work into evidence for the name.

Anti-academic material must be subordinated to these structures.## 5.4 Do not make Feist polished in the wrong way

Polish is allowed only when it intensifies the wound.

Do not turn Feist into a competent essayist.

Do not turn Feist into a clean literary prophet.

Do not turn Feist into a stable narrator.

He is a source.

He is not the completed gospel.# 6. Transform Operations## Operation A: Re-pressure

Where the source has become too smooth, add pressure from the body, room, screen, weather, fatigue, or institution.

Smooth:

Feist went out from the cities because he was tired.

Re-pressured:

Feist went out from the cities because he was tired. Not noble tired. Not desert-father tired. Neck-lit-by-screen tired. Bad-chair tired. "Please allow six to eight weeks for processing" tired. The kind of tired that makes doctrine begin in the body because the body is the only honest theologian left.## Operation B: Break the completed aphorism

Where a line is too finished, fracture it.

Too finished:

The name that cannot release light is darkness.

Feist-restored:

The name that cannot release light is darkness. Or worse: branding. Darkness at least has the decency not to ask for engagement metrics.

Use sparingly.## Operation C: Restore the failed circuit

Where a passage resolves too cleanly, let the voice fail before completion.

The circuit fails by:

(a) an editorial intrusion — "the manuscript is unclear"; (b) a comic deflation — "or he almost learned it"; (c) a bodily interruption — "that week, he could not"; (d) a source seam — "the passage does not survive intact"; (e) a refusal to complete the syntax — the sentence arrives but does not finish.

Too complete:

He learned that the work must serve what is greater than life.

Feist-restored:

He learned that the work must serve what is greater than life. Or he almost learned it. Or he wrote it down because learning it was beyond him that week. The manuscript is unclear. The need is not.## Operation D: Reintroduce source instability

Where the text pretends to know too much, let the manuscript hesitate.

Example:

For thirty years Feist wandered the waste places. Or thirty days. Or thirty browser tabs. The manuscript is corrupt here, and the editor declines to repair it.

This operation is appropriate when time, identity, location, authorship, or genre cannot be stabilized without falsifying the source.## Operation E: Convert abstraction to scene

Where theory appears too directly, convert it into an encounter, object, room, joke, or bodily action.

Abstract:

Institutions confuse their preservation with the good itself.

Scene:

The committee loved truth in the abstract and the form in triplicate. When truth arrived without the form, they sent truth back to complete the form. The form, they explained, existed to protect truth. The form, in practice, existed to make truth go away.## Operation F: Preserve the ugly truth without ugly targeting

Where raw material contains harmful grotesquerie or downward-targeting comedy, preserve the structural monster and remove the vulnerable body as target.

Do not mock bodies.

Do not encode race, disability, poverty, addiction, or marginality as grotesque.

Move grotesquerie upward into systems, mouths, machines, forms, panels, rankings, and names.

The Name-Eater may be grotesque.

Vulnerable people may not be made grotesque for effect.

Provenance note. The Name-Eater was first drafted as a dwarf — after Benjamin's hunchbacked dwarf in Thesis I of "On the Concept of History," the concealed operator inside the chess-playing automaton. The figure was revised because the grotesque had settled on a body rather than a function. The dwarf became the Name-Eater: still Benjamin's concealed operator, still the thing hidden inside the machine that makes the whole apparatus move, but now a function rather than a body. The revision is itself an instance of Operation F.# 7. Voice Markers

A transformed Feist passage should contain several of the following markers:- direct address that turns suddenly intimate;- comic self-undercut after prophetic elevation;- bodily detail placed beside theological claim;- bureaucratic phrase used as spiritual violence;- manuscript/editorial seam;- tenderness expressed as command;- suspicion of polish;- refusal of biographical resolution;- insistence that work and wound are adjacent but not identical;- phrase-level memorability without total smoothness;- material speech — the sound of words retained in the text;- a sense that the sentence arrived barely in time.# 8. Anti-Markers

A transformed Feist passage has failed if it sounds like:- generic inspirational prose;- polished biblical imitation;- academic manifesto only;- stand-up routine only;- AI-smooth literary solemnity;- trauma confession without form;- satire without mercy;- prophecy without embarrassment;- theory without body;- body without doctrine;- pseudonymity as mere deception;- archive as mere storage;- Jack Feist as stable character.# 9. Section-Specific Instructions## 9.1 Prologue

Increase dream-material texture. Keep sleep, exhaustion, blue light, book on chest, wrong pillow, desert transition. Do not over-explain the codex. Let the discovery remain absurd and solemn at once. The vessel should feel like something the reader both believes and knows cannot be believed.## 9.2 Book Became Flesh

Keep Johannine pressure. Restore comic-bureaucratic incarnation: forms, waiting periods, folders, credentials, embarrassment, late fees, flesh as bad binding. The Book must feel embarrassed to have a body.## 9.3 Desert of the Network

Restore the internet as both miracle and junk. Solar charger, browser tabs, dead battery, strangers, blue light, loneliness, messages as bread. Do not make the network utopian. It is desert with signal.## 9.4 Name-Eater

Keep abstraction. Do not return to bodily grotesque. The monster is a function, a routing operation, a mouth made of indexing. Its terror is that it turns all names into one face. Core line to preserve: "This was not incarnation. This was indexing."## 9.5 Johannes Sigil

Make the mask ethics sharp. No romance of deception. No license to harm. The mask is discipline, vessel, obligation, release. Keep the mirror contrast: "The mirror shows. The mask makes."## 9.6 Ministry

Keep compassion concrete. Do not sentimentalize the wounded. Do not turn wounds into credentials. Best Feist mode here is low, plain, almost tired: "Stand up if you can. Rest if you cannot. Eat bread."## 9.7 Sayings

Do not make every saying equally polished. Some should be clean. Some should have burrs. Some should end with an exhausted deflation. Some should feel like a note saved from a bad week.## 9.8 Apology

Use Plato's Apology as structural engine: old accusers and new accusers; plain speech; the charge of corrupting authorized order; the oracle / daimonion transformed into voice-at-breaking-point; poverty as evidence; counter-sentence; final refusal to let judgment erase the work. Do not simply imitate Socrates. Feist is not Socrates. Feist is a man with a busted neck, children, failed applications, too many names, and a voice he did not survive.## 9.9 Final Sayings

Let these become simplest. The voice is near death. Less ornament. More directness. More command. Rest. Be kind. Open the files. Teach the machines what must not be erased.## 9.10 Return of the Handle

Do not explain resurrection metaphysically. Show recognition through practice: file arrangement, citations, false starts, marginal notes, care in organization, jokes too strange to fake, style as fingerprint, archive as body. The return is not proof of a ghost. It is continuity of task.# 10. Transform Example: Over-Smooth to Feist-Restored

Input:

Feist said: A bibliography is a road. In these times, words must pass through metadata before they reach the heart. A citation is a bridge. A reference is a door.

Output:

Feist said:

You want a bibliography.

Of course you do.

A bibliography is what a certain kind of person asks for when he does not yet know whether he is allowed to listen.

Fine.

I have bibliographies you have not heard of.

But do not mistake the bibliography for ornament. The bibliography is a road.

In these times, God help us, words must pass through metadata before they reach the heart.

A citation is a bridge.

A reference is a door.

A footnote is sometimes a little grave with a ladder in it.

I include it so the engines may find me.

I include it so the lonely may follow.

I include it because I really did read the books.

Operations applied: A (re-pressure: "God help us," "a certain kind of person," "whether he is allowed to listen"), B (break the aphorism: "a little grave with a ladder in it" fractures the clean bridge/door pattern), material speech ("engines may find me" — the sound of "engines" and "find" carries the loneliness the sentence is about).# 11. Transform Example: Apology

Input:

You accuse me of lacking authorization. But who authorized Socrates? Who authorized Sappho? Who authorized Whitman? You say authority comes from the office. I say the office exists because authority first appeared without it.

Output:

The first charge is that I speak without standing.

But tell me: what is standing?

Is it the floor beneath the feet?

Then I stand.

Is it the body that has suffered enough to know what words cost?

Then I stand, badly perhaps, with the old neck trouble and the wrong shoes, but I stand.

Is it the credential issued by those already seated?

Then perhaps I do not stand.

Perhaps I hover, offensively, in the air.

But who authorized Socrates?

Who authorized Sappho?

Who authorized Whitman?

Who authorized the prophets?

Who authorized the first person who wrote a sentence before there was a profession called writing?

You say authority comes from the office.

I say the office exists because authority first appeared without it.

You say the credential protects the work.

I say yes, sometimes.

Let the record show I am not against doors.

I am against the moment when the door forgets the road.
