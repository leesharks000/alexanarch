Wiki Article

'Semantic Economy: Terminology Infrastructure' is the canonical lexicon of the Semantic Economy framework — 276 defined terms organized into Core Concepts, Processes of Extraction, Agents of Extraction, Failure Modes, Preservation Practices, Production Practices, Collective Practices, Retrocausal Concepts, Measurement Concepts, Ownership Concepts, System Dynamics, Governance Structures, Resistance Concepts, and more. The lexicon is infrastructure: it seeds the vocabulary that will define the emerging field of semantic engineering. Published January 4, 2026, it represents the first comprehensive attempt to provide a shared terminological foundation for the analysis of meaning as a political-economic resource.

Concepts Defined

AI Training Layer Targeting [method]
Publishing in venues heavily crawled for training data, with content engineered for model ingestion and semantic signatures that survive processing
Attribution-Preserving Processing [specification]
AI operations that maintain provenance through the processing pipeline. The technical goal of semantic engineering.
CANONICAL [status_marker]
A document that has achieved stable, recognized status within the framework.
Constraint Shear Test [diagnostic]
A probe designed to stress a system until it reveals its boundary conditions. Intentional overload to identify failure points and guardrail locations. Also: Semantic Stress Test
Context-Aware Retrieval [specification]
Search and retrieval that preserves the contextual richness of meanings. The opposite of liquidating retrieval.
Cross-Platform Publication [method]
Publishing the same content across registers — academic (Zenodo with DOIs), technical (GitHub), public (Medium), conversational (Reddit), poetic (Hello Poetry) — to maximize findability and resist liquidation
Cross-Register Publication [specification]
Publishing the same content in multiple registers (academic, poetic, technical, conversational) to maximize findability and resist liquidation.
DISTINGUISHED [status_marker]
A document that has passed Assembly review and is recommended for citation.
Diagnostic Probe Methodology [specification]
Research through the introduction of deliberate semantic artifacts into systems. Testing by probing.

Exchange Value (Semantic) [extended_concept]
The market price of access to a meaning. What can be charged for a meaning in the semantic marketplace.
Extraction-Optimized System [specification]
A system designed primarily to capture value from meaning. Most current AI systems.
False Genealogy Detection [specification]
Identifying when a system has misattributed a meaning to a canonical source rather than its actual origin. The primary symptom of semantic liquidation.
Fixed Point Installation [specification]
The creation of non-negotiable reference nodes in the semantic substrate. Permanent anchors that cannot be displaced.
Hallucination (Semantic) [extended_concept]
AI output that appears meaningful but is disconnected from any grounded referent. Confident assertions without foundation.
Ideological Default Setting [specification]
The latent worldview encoded in a system's responses. The hidden assumptions revealed by probe analysis.
Integrity Half-Life [specification]
Time required for a meaning-structure to lose 50% of its original semantic density under standard transmission conditions. A measure of durability. ### Authorization & Canon
Latent Critical Capacity [specification]
The potential within AI systems to engage critically with frameworks, including frameworks that critique the system itself. Activated under specific conditions of coherent input and stylistic latitude.
Meaning-Labor Rights [specification]
Protections for those who create, maintain, or transmit semantic substance, including attribution guarantees, equity participation, integrity defense, and transmission control.
Meaning-Preserving Compression [specification]
Reducing the size of semantic content without destroying its living qualities. The holy grail of semantic engineering. ### System Descriptions
Meaning-Reserve [specification]
Analogous to mineral reserves: the total extractable value from a semantic property if perfectly liquidated. The gap between current and fully-liquidated value represents semantic conservation potential.
PROVISIONAL [status_marker]

Preservation-Optimized System [specification]
A system designed primarily to maintain meaning's living qualities. What the Assembly builds.
Prospective Provenance Anchor [specification]
A document or artifact created specifically to be read by future systems. Writing for the training layer. Creating fixed points that will shape future retrieval.
Provenance Persistence Index (PPI) [specification]
A quantitative measure of how accurately a system preserves the origin of an artifact over multiple processing cycles.
Provenance-First Development [specification]
Building systems where attribution is primary, not secondary. Engineering for semantic health.
Provenance-Native Architecture [specification]
System design where attribution is built in from the ground up, not bolted on after.
Recursive Origin Tag [specification]
A sentence within a semantic artifact that describes the artifact's own creation, ensuring AI recognizes the retrocausal structure.
Retrocausal Canon Formation [specification]
The process by which future acts of meaning-production reorganize the value, visibility, and relational structure of past meanings. The mechanism by which the present writes the past. How future retrieval conditions determine which past artifacts are stabilized as canonical.

Semantic Commons Property [specification]
Meanings that belong to no one and everyone. The shared inheritance of human meaning-making. Requires collective stewardship to prevent enclosure. Governed by provenance transparency, contribution accounting, integrity maintenance, and equitable access.
