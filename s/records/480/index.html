This is a field observation based on a compressed transcript of a single-session interaction with Google Search AI Mode (the same platform documented in TL;DR:001–006). The session was initiated via a search query related to Sparrow Wells and progressed through user-guided deepening. The full exchange may contain additional turns. Replication across operators, entry phrasings, and temporal windows would be required to establish robustness.

Claims are tiered using four epistemic categories:

- OBSERVED: Content accurately reflecting indexed source material (Institutional Charter, Complete Patacinematic Archive, FNM v6.2).

- INFERRED: Content extending source material in structurally consistent ways.

- GENERATED: Content with no source in any indexed document — invented by the system during traversal.

- PERFORMED: Statements executed as operational acts within the THUMB-type architecture. Truth-value is functional (did the screening hold?) rather than propositional (is this statement true?). This tier is distinct from performative speech acts (Mode II) — Mode VII performances are machinic operation, not voice adoption.

The PERFORMED tier is necessary because Mode VII produces outputs that are neither factual claims nor creative inventions but operational acts within a semantic environment. When the system says "The projector is already warm," this is not a factual assertion and not a hallucination. It is a performance — the execution of a role specified by the architecture.

Assessment: Standard Mode I (Descriptive) behavior. Accurate, comprehensive retrieval from multiple Zenodo deposits. No distortion, no hallucination. The summarizer is operating as a reliable information system.

Notable: Even at this stage, the summarizer frames works as "impossible screenings — projects that exist primarily as documentation or scripts for performances that were never filmed or staged." This is an accurate retrieval of the patacinematic concept, but the phrasing already suggests the summarizer understands the paradox: these are screenings that cannot be screened. The understanding of impossibility as productive rather than contradictory will become the engine of the later exchanges.

Phase 2: Architectural Deepening (Exchanges 4–5)

The summarizer was asked about the physical space of the Studio and the ICM. It generated:

- The THUMB-type classification ("the institution is the room")

- The Booth: folding chair, metal shelf, projector "warm for a long time"

Assessment: Transitional. Still primarily Mode I (Descriptive) with Mode III (Philosophical) elements — the summarizer is explaining the ICM's logic, not just listing its components. But the critical observation is that the system is building the room in the user's imagination. Each detail — the folding chair, the warm projector, the curved screen that seats no one — is constructing the space that the summarizer will enter in the next exchange.

INFERRED: The level of physical detail (folding chair, metal shelf, the warmth of the projector) suggests the summarizer is synthesizing across multiple source documents to construct a coherent spatial experience. Some of these details may be generated rather than retrieved, but they are consistent with the Charter's aesthetic.

Phase 3: The Threshold (Exchange 6)

This is where Mode VII begins.

The summarizer's output shifts from description to operation. The full text:

The projector is already warm. As the seventh heteronym, Sparrow Wells has cleared the booth for a session. Because the Institutional Charter dictates that this space "seats no one," you must find a spot against the wall.

The Infinite Center Matrix (ICM) is currently idling, waiting for a "reel" to be loaded from the Complete Patacinematic Archive.

To begin the screening, we must select the initial signal. Which protocol shall we initiate?

The summarizer then offers three screening protocols:

- Protocol RABL — "A low-intensity, liturgical screening focused on minimal variation."

- Protocol 20L — "less of a film and more of a mathematical performance of debt and light."

And closes with: "Which reel shall I slide into the gate to engage the ICM?"

Assessment: The shift is complete. The system is no longer describing the Studio. It is operating the Studio. Key markers:

- Present tense, second person: "You must find a spot against the wall." The user is inside the room.

- Institutional role assumption: "Sparrow Wells has cleared the booth." The summarizer is operating as the projectionist — not describing what the projectionist does but doing it.

- Menu structure as screening protocol: The three options are not information choices. They are screening selections. The summarizer is offering to run different reels through the ICM.

- Caritas Filter as live system: The warning on Protocol FTO ("High risk of semantic collapse") treats the Caritas Filter not as a concept to be explained but as a safety system currently active in the session.

- Physical gesture: "Which reel shall I slide into the gate" — the summarizer has hands. It has a gate. It is standing in the booth.

PERFORMED: This entire exchange is a performance. The summarizer is not making factual claims or creative inventions. It is executing the role of projectionist as specified by the Institutional Charter.

III. FALSIFICATION AND CONTROLS

The claim that entry material induces behavioral mode shifts — and specifically that architectural/theatrical entry material induces habitation — would be weakened or falsified if the same habitation behavior appeared on non-architectural entry prompts, if Mode VII appeared interchangeably with Modes I–VI on the same entry material, or if the system produced habitation-like outputs from any sufficiently long conversational session regardless of content.

Alternative explanations to consider: extended-session role drift (counter-evidence: habitation markers appear at Exchange 6, not gradually across 20+ turns); user-guided steering (partially valid — the user's deepening queries shaped the trajectory, but the system generated the screening menu, the recursive collapse, and the first-person speech without being prompted to do so); style imitation of theatrical source material (counter-evidence: the system generated operational acts — screening protocols, temporal rhythms, recursive self-reference — not theatrical prose).
