Concepts Defined

Architectural hospitality [theoretical]
An institutional ethic: structures wait; they do not insist. Non-coercive availability without demand.
Institutional inflation [theoretical]
The failure mode of creating multiple 'Institutes' for every subdomain. One of three problems the UMBML moon-base architecture solves by domain consolidation.
Minimal force [theoretical]
An institutional ethic: threshold, not impact. Function operates by establishing conditions for crossing, not by overwhelming with effect.
Register confusion [theoretical]
The failure mode of mixing technical specifications with poetics-institute work. One of three problems the UMBML moon-base architecture solves by separating registers across distinct institutional homes.
Terrestrial capture [theoretical]
The failure mode in which conventional institutions can be audited, defunded, or colonized. UMBML's moon-base distance is the architectural response: not findable, not auditable.
Transparent construction [theoretical]
An institutional ethic: the institutional fiction is legible as fiction. Names its own construction openly; honesty about being constructed is more durable than pretense to natural authority.
University Moon Base Media Lab [institution]
The technical/operational research institution within the NH-OS framework. Handles experimental, computational, and infrastructural work that requires distance from terrestrial institutional capture. 'On the moon' — cannot be visited, audited, or colonized.
