I. Definition

"I Hereby Abolish Money" (phrase): A performative utterance that functions as entry point into post-money economic thinking.

The phrase is deliberately impossible—no individual can abolish money through declaration—yet its utterance opens a pedagogical and theoretical space for examining what money actually does and how those functions might be fulfilled otherwise.

II. Theoretical Function

The phrase operates on multiple levels:

1. Performative Failure

Like saying "I hereby make it rain," the utterance cannot accomplish what it declares. This failure is productive: it exposes the gap between speech and institutional power.

Money is not a thing that can be abolished by fiat. It is a coordination system maintained by millions of interlocking practices. The impossibility of the declaration reveals the nature of what it names.

2. Diagnostic Trigger

The phrase forces the question: "Abolish it and replace it with what?"

This question cannot be answered without first understanding what money does. The phrase is a diagnostic that reveals money's functions by demanding their replacement.

3. Pedagogical Entry

The phrase opens space for the Money-Function Test and post-money coordination frameworks. It is a door into a room of serious inquiry, entered through apparent absurdity.

4. Semantic Territory

The phrase establishes conceptual real estate for alternative economic thinking. It is a flag planted. It is now searchable, indexable, retrievable. The territory exists because we declared it.

This is an effective act: an utterance that builds the conditions for its own realization through propagation.

III. The Money-Function Test

When someone says "I hereby abolish money," the correct response is:

"Abolish it and replace it with what?"

Money performs five functions:

Function

What It Does

Memory

Records who contributed what

Coordination

Aligns activity across strangers

Allocation

Determines who gets access to what

Settlement

Resolves obligations and disputes

Incentivization

Motivates participation

Any post-money system must address all five without reintroducing currency properties:

  • Transferability
  • Accumulability
  • Fungibility
  • Convertibility
  • Hoardability

    • If your "alternative to money" has all five currency properties, you have not abolished money. You have renamed it.

    Generative: Opens rather than closes inquiry. The impossibility of the declaration is the beginning, not the end.

    Memorable: Functions as conceptual handle for complex framework. You will remember this phrase.

    Testable: Immediately invites the question of what replaces money's functions. The test is built into the utterance.

    V. Related Concepts

    Post-Money Operator Stack (PMOS): Technical specification for coordination without universal equivalent. Five operators that replace money's five functions without reintroducing currency properties.

    Twenty-Dollar Loop: Pedagogical game revealing money as coordination fiction. Pass a $20 bill around a circle; it "pays for" $100+ of goods; no wealth was created. Money is agreement, not substance.

    Semantic Labor: The cognitive-linguistic work of producing meaning. Work that cannot be paid without being destroyed—because payment requires flattening context into fungible units.

    Effective Act: Utterance that builds conditions for its own realization. "I hereby abolish money" is an effective act: by propagating, it creates the conceptual space for its own possibility.
