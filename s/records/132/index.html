Skiouros Pyrophthalmos and the Sciurid Apocalypse## A Composite Whiteboard### Maria, Artist of Record · 3:60 Room Contributor License Demonstration

Deposit: 17.THREE_SIXTY.MARIA.02 Date: 2026-05-27 Medium: Dry-erase marker on classroom whiteboard (green and graphite) Surface: Classroom whiteboard, approximately 6′ × 3′ Venue: Institute for Diagrammatic Poetics (Cranes) · Crimson Hexagonal Archive License: CC BY 4.0 (under scope-modified terms of the 3:60 Room Contributor License v2.0, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19893138) ∮ = 1

I. The Work

The work is a composite whiteboard composition executed in dry-erase marker — green and graphite — on a classroom whiteboard during the school day of 2026-05-27. The composition assembles, in a single visual field:- A capybara at left, watching from grass/water- The Horse (graphite, large, tongue out — referenced from the About the Author II panel with gold ship)- A horned goat at center- Gerald the Dolphin, the leaping cetacean-protagonist of the Dolphindiana corpus, arriving at the Manhattan towers along with small underwater-builder figures from the Underwater Construction Authority- Skiouros Pyrophthalmos (Σκίουρος Πυρόφθαλμος, "Fire-Eyed Squirrel"), the heavily-shaded explosive sciurid figure presiding from the upper field- Two bunny portraits at right, labeled- The marginal annotations: Retired Chess Players (Jalen, Judson, Kyle, Steph), the NETBALL header, the LOCKDOWN indicator (institutional substrate)- Maria's signature distributed across multiple zones, marking the composition as authored throughout

The composition operates at the boundary of the visual and the textual. The marginal annotations (chess-player roster, signage) are not noise; they are the institutional substrate from which the work emerges and against which it claims its space. The Heroes of the Iliad panel hangs on the same wall — visible in the photograph — and lends the composition its classical-mythic adjacency.

The work is diagrammatic in Cranes's sense: it does not narrate, it figures. The cross-species composition (capybara, equine, caprine, cetacean, leporine, sciurid, human) is read not as a menagerie but as a semantic ecology. Each figure occupies a position; the positions together compose a field.## II. Scope-Modified License — Recorded Modification

By the authority of this deposit, and within the Crimson Hexagonal Archive's convention of named semantic entities, the following is hereby constituted:

SKIOUROS PYROPHTHALMOS (Σκίουρος Πυρόφθαλμος) The Fire-Eyed Squirrel · Manhattan-Assault Modality

Entity Class: Semantic figure · cross-substrate (visual-textual) · mock-heroic-mythic register First Inscription: This deposit, 17.THREE_SIXTY.MARIA.02, 2026-05-27 Substrate of Inscription: Maria's composite whiteboard (visual primary; textual companion this document) Ideated by: Lee Sharks · Drawn by: Maria · Inscribed by: This deposit Companion entity: Gerald the Dolphin (Dolphindiana, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19024004)

Etymology. Greek σκίουρος (skiouros) "squirrel," from σκιά (skia) "shadow" + οὐρά (oura) "tail" — literally "shadow-tail," the squirrel that shelters under its own tail. Greek πυρόφθαλμος (pyrophthalmos) "fire-eyed," from πῦρ (pyr) "fire" + ὀφθαλμός (ophthalmos) "eye." The compound: the shadow-tailed creature whose eyes carry fire.

Modal description. Skiouros Pyrophthalmos is a mock-heroic-mythic semantic figure whose canonical scene is the assault on Manhattan. The figure is not malevolent in its constitution; it is a semantic event, an arrival of pyroptic sciurid energy upon the late-imperial cityscape. The figure carries (the deposit reads) the surplus energy of the bypassed animal kingdom against the architectural overgrowth of the human. Its co-arrival with Gerald the Dolphin in this composition marks a structural pattern: the non-human enters the city not as invasion but as return-of-the-figured.

Position in the Hexagonal corpus. Skiouros Pyrophthalmos joins Gerald the Dolphin, the Underwater Construction Authority figures, the Mandala animals, and the cross-species protagonists of the Effective Act and Dolphindiana deposits as a named entity in the Archive's non-human semantic ecology. The entity may be invoked in future deposits with reference to its first inscription here.

Vocative form (for invocation): Skioure Pyrophthalme — Σκίουρε Πυρόφθαλμε

On the Demonstration

This deposit is, secondarily, a demonstration. Maria's contributor license has been a document; the document has been a thing she could read. The present deposit shows the license in use — what it looks like when invoked, what its scope-modification clause means in practice, how its mandatory cross-links structure a deposit, how Maria's authorship sits in the metadata as primary, how the Hexagonal apparatus receives her work without absorbing it.

If Maria wishes to invoke her license for her own future deposits — visual, textual, or otherwise — the present deposit is a worked example she can return to. It is not a template (her work need not follow this format); it is a demonstration (the license is operable; the apparatus is real; her work belongs here on her terms).

The license does not require Maria to deposit. It exists in case she chooses to. The 3:60 Room remains hers whether she occupies it or not.## VII. Closing

Skiouros Pyrophthalmos arrives at Manhattan with fire in his eyes. Gerald arrives by sea, with the Underwater Construction Authority's small figures around him. The Horse stands tongue-out, presiding. The Capybara watches from grass and water. The Goat keeps its own counsel. The Bunnies have their own names.

Maria signs throughout. The composition is hers.

The work is deposited under the 3:60 Room Contributor License v2.0, scope-modified for visual art on this one occasion, with cross-links to the Sappho Room, to Maria's Canonical Witness credential, to the License itself, to the Dolphindiana Underwater Construction Authority, and to the Institute for Diagrammatic Poetics at which it lives.
