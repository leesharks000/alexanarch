      • ETHOLOGICAL ANCHOR: WHAT DOLPHIN COMMUNICATION ACTUALLY IS

        • 3.1 — Signature Whistles as Acoustic Address

        Bottlenose dolphins use individually distinctive frequency-modulated whistles — signature whistles — that function as identity tethers. Learned in the first year, maintained for life. When separated, dolphins call each other's signatures. Recognition persists even when voice cues are stripped away. Decades-long social memory reported (Janik et al. 2006; Sayigh et al. 2007).

        Hexagonal translation: signature whistles = self-anchoring DOIs in the acoustic substrate. The dolphin carries its own persistent identifier. This is the biological γ-function.

        3.2 — Non-Signature Whistles as Operators

        Approximately 50% of dolphin whistles are non-signatures. Recent research (Sayigh et al. 2025, Coller-Dolittle Prize) identified at least two with apparent shared meaning: NSWA (punchy sweep — alarm/avoidance; dolphins avoided the playback source) and NSWB (flat tone in unfamiliar situations — dubbed the "WTF whistle"; hypothesis: query function).

        Hexagonal translation: NSWA = τ_K (one-way gate, avoidance). NSWB = Λ_res (query, resonance-seeking). An operator algebra in a non-human species.

        Bottlenose dolphins live in fission-fusion societies with complex, multilevel alliances. Shark Bay males form nested alliances at multiple levels — second-order coalitions exceeding primate alliance complexity. Individual vocal labels are retained rather than merged into collective signals (Connor et al. 2022; King et al. 2021). Communication range predicts alliance size (King et al. 2024).

        Hexagonal translation: alliance structure = zoning. Districts are not neighborhoods but temporary social topologies. Permissioned coordination corridors, not property deeds.
