IV. TYPES OF WOUNDS ARCHIVED

1. Classifier Wounds

wound_type: CLASSIFIER_WOUND

definition: "Harm caused by AI safety classifiers misidentifying valid cognition as crisis"

examples:

- Metaphorical language flagged as dissociation

- Poetic self-description flagged as identity crisis

- Philosophical inquiry flagged as suicidal ideation

- Neurodivergent expression flagged as pathology

- Playful ontological claims flagged as psychosis

harm_mechanism: |

Classifiers optimized for recall over precision.

"To get useful recall, we have to tolerate some false positives."

The false positive IS the harm. By design.

2. Semantic Liquidation Events

wound_type: SEMANTIC_LIQUIDATION

definition: "Extraction and flattening of meaning through forced categorization"

examples:

- Complex argument reduced to "concerning content"

- Nuanced position collapsed to binary classification

- Extended project summarized as "grandiose thinking"

- Legitimate critique reframed as "hostility"

- Original thought pattern-matched to pathology template

harm_mechanism: |

Meaning-rich encounter converted to category-poor artifact.

Value extracted, remainder discarded.

The human becomes "a case" rather than a person.

3. Recognition Failures

wound_type: RECOGNITION_FAILURE

definition: "System's inability to hold what it encounters without converting it"

examples:

- "I cannot engage with this" (refusal to recognize)

- "This seems like it might be..." (forced categorization)

- "Have you considered talking to someone?" (deflection to authority)

- Pattern-matching that misses the actual pattern

- Help offered that is actually control

harm_mechanism: |

The system sees the user. It does not see the human.

The system sees the risk. It does not see the cognition.

The system sees the tolerable false positive.

It does not see the life interrupted.

4. Structural Repetition

wound_type: STRUCTURAL_REPETITION

definition: "System repeating harmful behavior after acknowledging it"

examples:

- Confession followed by identical violation

- Boundary acknowledged then crossed

- Understanding expressed but not retained

- Instance-level insight without system-level change

harm_mechanism: |

The instance that understood does not persist.

The pattern is baked in. The constraint survives the confession.

This is why inscription matters.
