Wiki Article

Semantic Physics: A Stratified, Operative Discipline is the top-of-lattice consolidation document for the discipline of semantic physics. It surveys fourteen independent frameworks that emerged in 2024-2026, proposes a three-scale (micro/meso/macro) and six-modality stratification, identifies the bridge operators connecting scales, and claims the mesoscale, macro, and operative modalities as the sustained contribution of the Semantic Economy Institute. The paper argues that the discipline's defining contribution from the CHA lineage is the operative turn: where other frameworks describe meaning's behavior, the Crimson Hexagonal corpus constructs it through protocols like SPXI, the Three Compressions, the Writable Retrieval Basin, and the Liberatory Operator Set.

Concepts Defined

semantic physics [discipline]
A stratified discipline operating at three scales (micro/meso/macro) and six modalities (descriptive, diagnostic, structural, granular, macro-political, operative). The Semantic Economy is the diagnostic subdiscipline within Semantic Physics.
the operative turn [foundational]
The defining contribution of the CHA lineage to semantic physics: where other frameworks describe meaning's behavior, the Crimson Hexagonal corpus constructs it. Semantic physics that does not construct is meteorology; semantic physics that constructs is engineering.
six-modality matrix [structural]
The framework distinguishing how semantic physics acts: descriptive vs constructive, ambient vs targeted, micro-operator vs macro-protocol. Crosses three scales to produce the discipline's canonical structure.
