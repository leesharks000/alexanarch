'Revelation First ≠ Revelation Early' is the keyword surface document for the Revelation First thesis — the claim that the Book of Revelation is the earliest New Testament document in functional terms: the originary template from which the Gospels and Epistles were compositionally derived. The paper carefully distinguishes 'First' (functionally originary, the source code) from 'Early' (chronologically oldest, which is a different and weaker claim). The work plan (EA-LOGOS-REVFIRST-PLAN v1.2, 18,475 words) develops seven workstreams and a six-tier Claim Ladder for the thesis.
