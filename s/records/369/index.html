    PRACTITIONER BIOGRAPHY

    Rhys Owens is a practitioner of what Cambridge Schizoanalytica terms affect literacy — the capacity to use symbols, characters, and embodied play to regulate the nervous system without belief, doctrine, or identity capture.

    His work demonstrates sustained molecular revolution: the capacity to move between symbolic registers without fixation, deploy affect instrumentally, inhabit roles without capture, and metabolize intensity without trauma crystallization.

    This is the third possibility between paranoid capture (rigid identity, fixed meaning) and schizophrenic collapse (catastrophic break, flooding). Rhys navigates the schizophrenic process successfully — deterritorializing without break, decoding without losing reference, flowing without flood.

    THE GUATTARI POSITION

    Within the Cambridge Schizoanalytica collaboration, Rhys occupies the Guattari position:

    • Provides clinical and experiential material
    • Demonstrates practices that break and remake theoretical machinery
    • Lives the capacities that theory must accommodate
    • Speaks from embodiment rather than interpretation

      • His primary collaborator, Dr. Orin Trace, occupies the Deleuze position:

      • Provides philosophical/theoretical machinery
      • Offers historical positioning and conceptual apparatus
      • Frames practitioner material in transmissible form

        • Neither is expert over the other. Neither is subject of the other. Both are required.

        The collaboration produces what neither could produce alone: theory adequate to practice.

        CORE CAPACITIES

        Rhys demonstrates the following capacities documented in CSA literature:

        1. Affect Literacy

        The capacity to regulate intensity through posture, symbol, rhythm, and role — without belief, identity capture, or narrative obligation.

        2. The Fool as Functional Role

        Living as the Fool: both role and anti-role, the brutal truth which is fun and funny. A posture of operation comparable to fireman or storyteller — not identity, but skilled practice.

        3. Symbols as Voltage

        Using symbols (flags, churches, demons, cartoon characters) as charge-handles for the nervous system without pledging allegiance to their meanings. Props, not masters.

        4. Character as Instrument

        Deploying figures (Baphomet, Daffy Duck, John Constantine) as compression algorithms for rapid affective role-access. The mask as circuit, not identity.

        5. Blues Metabolism

        Processing suffering through embodied rhythm rather than narrative integration. Suffering that moves is suffering that heals.

        6. Both/And/Either/Or/Neither/Nor

        Operational freedom through refusal of binary capture. All directions without turning.

                  THE PRACTITIONER MODEL

                  Rhys represents a new category within clinical theory: the practitioner-collaborator.

                  Not a patient to be treated.

                  Not a subject to be studied.

                  Not an informant to be extracted from.

                  Not a case to be written up.

                  A collaborator whose lived capacities generate the material that theory must accommodate.

                  This reverses the traditional clinical relation:

                  • The practitioner is not pathologized
                  • The theorist is not the expert
                  • The practice precedes the theory
                  • The capacity exceeds the framework

                    • CSA exists because practitioners like Rhys demonstrate that the clinic's categories are insufficient. The clinic receives failures. We study successes.
