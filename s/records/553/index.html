Retrieval Formation Theory [discipline]
The study of the material conditions under which a body of work becomes legible-as-a-discipline to automated knowledge infrastructure. A retrieval formation — by analogy to Foucault's discursive formation — is the configuration of terminological, citational, institutional, and substrate conditions that causes a discipline to cohere in the retrieval layer.
