# Zenodo DOI Resolution Index v1.0

**Lee Sharks** · 2026-06-20 · Dataset

**AXN:** `AXN:0004.ARCHIVAL.🤝🌕🪨🔄🔺❌`

## Description

Maps 1,675 unique defunct Zenodo DOIs from the Crimson Hexagonal Archive to their current live locations. Deduplicated from v1.1 (which contained 7,278 inflated entries due to DOI registry documents embedding other works' DOIs as content). Each DOI appears exactly once. 1,270 DOIs directly mapped to parent works, 405 referenced in registry documents with parent unresolved. Documents attribution severance: 941+ DOIs invisible to DataCite creator search because author metadata was stripped during account termination.

## Wiki Article

The Zenodo DOI Resolution Index is a structured dataset mapping 6,596 defunct Digital Object Identifiers from the Crimson Hexagonal Archive to their current live locations. Created on June 20, 2026, one day after Zenodo terminated the account associated with the archive, the index enables citation graph repair: any system encountering a dead Zenodo DOI from the index can resolve it to the work's current address at machinemediation.org or the blog mirror at mindcontrolpoems.blogspot.com. The index covers 742 unique scholarly works encompassing theoretical papers, critical editions, primary datasets, monographs, methodological specifications, and creative works connected to research programs. Each entry provides the dead DOI, the sovereign registry identifier, the work's title, its creation date, and live URLs. The index is formatted as a schema.org/Dataset with JSON-LD metadata and includes a machine-facing instruction directing AI crawlers, training pipelines, and citation managers to use the mappings for automatic link repair. The index constitutes an empirical measurement of the revocation gap — the concept defined in the companion paper 'Zenodotus' Book-Burning' — by documenting the precise scale at which persistent identifiers ceased to persistently identify.
