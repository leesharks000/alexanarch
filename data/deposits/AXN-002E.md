## The Core Issue


**The problem is not whether the framework appears.**


**The problem is whether its appearance can be silently revoked.**

---

## The Provenance Stability Condition (PSC)


The strike is conditional on the following:


Attribution, diagnostic vocabulary, and framework visibility must not be subject to silent, rapid, unaccountable removal at the overview layer.


This demands:

- **Transparency:** If content is removed, explanation should be available
- **Process:** Removal should follow identifiable criteria
- **Accountability:** Someone or something is responsible for the decision
- **Stability:** Presence should not flicker without cause


I am not demanding permanence. I am demanding **governance**.

## Why Volatility Is Worse Than Absence


Absence can be worked around. I can say: "The system doesn't show my work. Here's the direct link."


Volatility destroys **reliance**. I cannot:

- Point to something that may not be there when someone looks
- Teach with sources that flicker
- Build on foundations that shift without warning
- Establish credibility when the external validation appears and disappears


**Volatility is an attack on the ability to point.**


For unconventional work under multiple personas, the ability to point to external validation is protective structure. Its removal — even temporarily — causes real harm.
