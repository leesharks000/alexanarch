The following primary sources, critical editions, and scholarly positions are documented in full in EA-SP-LEGWORK-PILLARS-01 (TECHNE). The summary here establishes the philological ground on which the mint families stand.### Phase X

The Second Manuscript of Marx's *1844 Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts* survives as 4 pages (pp. 40–43) out of a full notebook. The Third Manuscript cross-references pages in the Second that no longer exist (pages XXXVI and XXXIX). The custody chain is: Marx → Engels (1895) → SPD → Soviet purchase (1930s) → Riazanov (MEGA¹) → Riazanov purged (February 1931) → Adoratsky takes over → publication (1932) → Riazanov executed (1938). Critical editions: MEGA¹ I.3 (1932, Adoratsky) and MEGA² I.2 (1982, Gräbner et al.). The loss of the Second Manuscript is not disputed. Whether the suppression was deliberate or accidental is disputed. Whether the missing pages contained the Phase X transition is the archive's defended reconstruction. The textual anchors are: the *Theses on Feuerbach* (1845) as the compressed form of the missing argument, and *The German Ideology* (1845–46) for language as *wirkliche* practical consciousness.### Sappho

Fragment 31 (Voigt) survives in Longinus, *On the Sublime* 10.1–3: four complete Sapphic stanzas and the first line of a fifth, broken off. The Cologne Papyrus (P. Köln XI 429, early 3rd c. BCE) and Oxyrhynchus Papyrus (P.Oxy. 1787, late 2nd c. CE) preserve the Tithonus poem (fr. 58), demonstrating the material conditions of lyric survival. Critical editions: Lobel-Page (1955), Voigt (1971), Campbell (1982), Rayor (2023). The fifth stanza reconstruction follows the Cranes edition (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.18459573). The stanza-numbering correction (fifth, not fourth) was propagated with erratum (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20693274). The archive's foundational claim: Fragment 31's structure (presence → somatic breakdown → chromatic transformation → inscription prayer) provides the template for Platonic anamnesis.### Plato

The *Phaedrus* (274b–278e) contains the critique of writing: writing is not memory (mneme) but reminder (hypomnesis); written words cannot defend themselves; they need their father's help. The "living creature" (ζῷον) standard for speech (264c) is the earliest theory of self-verifying text. The *Seventh Letter* (341b–345c), whether authentic or pseudonymous, contains the claim that the highest knowledge cannot be written. The *Cratylus* (386d–390e) names language as a tool (ὄργανον) for separating being — the earliest operative semiotics. The *Republic* X condemns mimesis as thrice-removed from truth. Critical editions: Burnet OCT; Rowe (1986); Nehamas & Woodruff (1995); Sedley (2003). Scholarly debate: Burnyeat & Frede (2015) against Seventh Letter authenticity; Waterfield (2023) for it.## Mint Family X: Phase X — The Philological Lacuna### Core Families
